3 # Unified Post-Processing (UPP)
4 
5 The Unified Post Processor (UPP) software package is a software
6 package designed to generate useful products from raw model
7 output. The UPP is currently used in operations with the Global
8 Forecast System (GFS), GFS Ensemble Forecast System (GEFS), North
9 American Mesoscale (NAM), Rapid Refresh (RAP), High Resolution Rapid
10 Refresh (HRRR), Short Range Ensemble Forecast (SREF), Hurricane WRF
11 (HWRF) applications, and is also used in Unified Forecasting System
12 (UFS) applications. The UPP provides the capability to compute a
13 variety of diagnostic fields and interpolate to pressure levels or
14 other vertical coordinates. UPP also incorporates the Joint Center for
15 Satellite Data Assimilation (JCSDA) Community Radiative Transfer Model
16 (CRTM) to compute model derived brightness temperature (TB) for
17 various instruments and channels. This additional feature enables the
18 generation of a number of simulated satellite products including GOES
19 products. Output from the UPP is in National Weather Service (NWS) and
20 World Meteorological Organization (WMO) GRIB2 format and can be used
21 directly by visualization, plotting, or verification packages, or for
22 further downstream post-processing, e.g. statistical post-processing
23 techniques. Examples of UPP products include:
24 
25 - T, Z, humidity, wind, cloud water, cloud ice, rain, and snow on pressure levels
26 - SLP, shelter level T, humidity, and wind fields
27 - Precipitation-related fields
28 - PBL-related fields
29 - Severe weather products (e.g. CAPE, Vorticity, Wind shear)
30 - Radiative/Surface fluxes
31 - Cloud related fields
32 - Aviation products
33 - Radar reflectivity products
34 - Satellite look-alike products
35 
36 **Prerequisites**
37 The UPP requires certain NCEPLIBS packages to be installed via the spack-stack project. For instructions on installing these packages as a bundle via spack-stack, see: https://spack-stack.readthedocs.io/en/latest/. The UPP/modulefiles directory indicates which package versions are used and supported on Level 1 systems.

39 Community support for the Unified Forecast System (UFS) UPP in FV3-based applications is provided by the
40 Earth Prediction Innovation Center (EPIC). Community support for the UPP with WRF is no longer available.
